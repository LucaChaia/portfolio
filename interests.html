Interests & Extracurriculars

Outside of class, I have been most drawn to communities and projects that combine technical work with leadership, collaboration, and real-world impact. These experiences have helped shape how I approach both learning and building.

Blueprint Boulder

Blueprint Boulder was one of my favorite ways to use technical skills in a practical setting. I enjoyed being part of a team that built software for nonprofits, because the work had a clear purpose beyond the classroom. It pushed me to think not just about writing code, but about building tools that were actually useful, maintainable, and meaningful for the people using them.

Data Buffs

Data Buffs gave me the opportunity to combine technical curiosity with leadership. I enjoyed helping create a space where students could learn about data science in a hands-on and approachable way. More than anything, I liked being part of a community that encouraged people to ask questions, build confidence, and explore how data can be used to better understand real problems.

Teaching & Workshops

One thing I have found especially rewarding is helping other students feel more comfortable with technology. Through workshops and technical outreach, I have enjoyed breaking down intimidating topics into steps that feel manageable and practical. Teaching others has made me a better communicator and a more thoughtful builder, because it forces me to focus on clarity, patience, and what is actually useful to someone just getting started.
